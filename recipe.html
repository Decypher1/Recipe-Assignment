Nigerian Jollof Rice

The pride of West Africa this vibrant, smoky, and flavorful rice dish is a staple at Nigerian parties, weddings, funerals and family gatherings. Perfect with fried plantain, grilled chicken, or fish.

Recipe Information

Prep time: 20 minutes

Cook time: 45 minutes

Servings: 6 people

Difficulty: Beginner

Ingredients

  • 3 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 6 large tomatoes (blended)
  • 4 red bell peppers (blended)
  • 2 medium onions (1 blended, 1 sliced)
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (blended)
  • 3 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • 1 tsp curry powder
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Blend tomatoes, red bell peppers, scotch bonnet peppers, and one onion until smooth.
  2. Per boil and wash the rice
  3. Heat oil in a large pot, add sliced onions until fragrant.
  4. Add tomato paste and fry for 2 minutes to remove raw taste.
  5. Pour in blended mixture, season with thyme, curry powder, and fry for 10â€“15 minutes.
  6. Add chicken stock, salt, and rice. Stir well to combine.
  7. Reduce heat, cover with foil and pot lid, and steam-cook until rice is tender.
  8. Fluff rice gently and serve hot with fried plantain, chicken, or fish.

Tips

For authentic smoky flavor, allow the rice to slightly toast at the bottom of the pot during the last 5 minutes of cooking. Stir gently to avoid breaking the grains.
